\begin{block}{1 \quad Motivation}
Masked diffusion LLMs generate text by iteratively unmasking tokens
\emph{in parallel}, enabling any-order planning.
Yet current inference relies on \textbf{static confidence thresholds}
and \textbf{fixed block schedules}:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep3pt
  \item Uniform compute regardless of position difficulty.
  \item No adaptive self-correction of mis-drafted tokens.
  \item Per-task hyperparameter tuning required.
\end{itemize}

\medskip
Jazbec et al.\ (2025) trained a lightweight RL policy for \emph{unmasking}
via cold-start GRPO, but this covers only unmasking and collapses in our
dual-model setting (10--14\% accuracy after 150 RL steps; see \S4).

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Goal:} a lightweight policy jointly controlling \textbf{unmasking}
($u_t$) and \textbf{remasking} ($r_t$) on top of a speculative dual-mode
inference loop — trained stably without cold-start RL instability.
\end{block}

\begin{block}{2 \quad Dual-Mode MoE Architecture}
LLaDA\,2.1-mini is a 16B-parameter MoE with 256 routed experts per layer.
AOAE exposes \textbf{two operational modes of the same frozen model}:

\bigskip
\namedpara{primaryblue}{Hard-routed drafter (fast)}{%
  Standard top-8 routing
  $\;\Rightarrow\;${\bfseries ${\sim}$1.4B active params.}\\[4pt]
  Cheap draft microsteps proposing token values for all masked positions.
}

\namedpara{accentred}{Soft-routed verifier (expressive)}{%
  Temperature-widened routing to top-16 experts
  $\;\Rightarrow\;${\bfseries all 16B params participate.}\\[4pt]
  Periodic validation; applies PRISM-style keep\,/\,$\mask$ decisions
  at each verifier event.
  Accepts position $k$ when drafter--verifier argmax agree
  $\ge\delta_{\mathrm{acc}}$; remasks otherwise.
}

Both modes share the \emph{same frozen weights}: routing toggled in-place,
halving memory versus loading two separate model copies.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Inference loop (per verifier event $t$):}
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep2pt
  \item \textbf{Draft}: hard-routed pass; policy decides which masked
        positions to unmask ($u_t^k{=}1$).
  \item \textbf{Verify}: soft-routed pass; low-agreement positions
        get $r_t^k{=}1$ (remask to $\mask$).
  \item \textbf{Commit}: accepted positions join the response;
        rejected positions re-enter the draft pool.
\end{enumerate}
\end{block}

\begin{block}{3 \quad Lightweight Policy (${\sim}465$K params)}
\textbf{PhaseAV2Policy}: 1-layer bidirectional transformer
($d{=}128$, 4 heads).  Per-position scalar inputs:

\vspace{0.3cm}
\begin{center}
\setlength{\tabcolsep}{7pt}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Feature} & \textbf{Dim} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
Mask indicator $m_t^k$  & 1 & Position currently masked? \\
Step ratio $t/T$         & 1 & Diffusion progress \\
Max confidence $c_t^k$   & 1 & $\max_v p_\theta(v\mid\mathbf{y}_t)$ \\
Agreement $\alpha_t^k$   & 1 & Drafter $\leftrightarrow$ verifier argmax match \\
PRISM score $q_t^k$      & 1 & Verifier quality; 0 on drafter steps \\
\midrule
\textbf{Total (V4)} & \textbf{5} & Scalar-only \\
\textbf{Total (V5)} & \textbf{5+emb} & + $h_{\mathrm{final}}$ for $r_t$ head \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Active heads:}
$u_t^k \!\sim\! \mathrm{Ber}(\sigma(f_\phi^u\!(s_t)^k))$ (unmask),
$r_t^k \!\sim\! \mathrm{Ber}(\sigma(f_\phi^r\!(s_t)^k))$ (remask).
Cache and access heads disabled; reserved for future work.
\end{block}

\begin{block}{4 \quad Two-Stage Training Pipeline}

\namedpara{accentgreen}{Stage 1 — Supervised Warmstart (dense BCE)}{%
  Run speculative rollouts with the heuristic policy.
  For each position $k$ per trajectory step, derive binary labels from
  future verifier outcomes:
  \[
    \ell_t^{u,k} = \mathbf{1}[\text{verifier would accept}],\quad
    \ell_t^{r,k} = \mathbf{1}[\text{verifier would reject}].
  \]
  Train with per-position binary cross-entropy — \textbf{dense signal,
  dense gradients}.\\[5pt]
  \textbf{500 steps, ${\sim}$2.5\,h on $1{\times}$H200.}
  Final BCE loss ${\approx}0.002$; smooth convergence, no instability.
}

\namedpara{primaryblue}{Stage 2 — GRPO (Reinforcement Learning)}{%
  Initialise from warmstart checkpoint.
  Sample $G{=}4$ rollouts per prompt; compute group-mean advantages;
  update with clipped PPO surrogate ($\varepsilon{=}0.2$).
  \textbf{No expert steering. No KL penalty.}\\[5pt]
  \textbf{Reward} (multiplicative — prevents reward hacking):
  \[
    R \;=\; r(\hat{\mathbf{y}},\mathbf{y}^*)
          \cdot(1 - \mathrm{eff\_flops})^\alpha
          \;-\; \beta\,\tfrac{\text{remask}}{L}
          \;-\; w_u\,f_{\text{unresolved}}
  \]
  where $\mathrm{eff\_flops} = \mathrm{used\_steps}/T$, $\alpha{=}1$.\\[5pt]
  \textbf{200 steps (V4); $4{\times}$H200, DP=4.}
}

\end{block}

\begin{block}{5 \quad Key Insight: Why Warmstart Enables GRPO}

\begin{center}
\setlength{\tabcolsep}{7pt}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.35}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
 & \textbf{Cold-start RL (V3)} & \textbf{Warmstart $\to$ RL (V4/V5)} \\
\midrule
Initialisation & Random & Near-good solution \\
Training signal & Sparse reward only & Dense BCE $\to$ sparse reward \\
Expert steering needed? & Yes (still fails) & \textbf{No} \\
Training stability & Oscillates; stalls & Smooth convergence \\
GSM8K accuracy & 10--14\% & \textbf{86\%} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\medskip
Jazbec et al.\ tried conditioning the policy on hidden state $h_t$ from
cold-start RL and found it \emph{hurt}: sparse end-of-episode reward
cannot navigate high-dimensional hidden-state space.
\textbf{PRISM} (Kim et al., 2025) showed the same hidden states
\emph{are} learnable via dense supervised labels.
Our warmstart is structurally analogous to PRISM fine-tuning: dense
per-position BCE labels solve credit assignment first; GRPO then
refines for the global correctness$\,{\times}\,$speed objective.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Analogy:} cold-start RL on $h_t$ is like training a medical
classifier from scratch on 100 labels; warmstart is like fine-tuning
an ImageNet-pretrained backbone — the prior structure does the heavy lifting.
\end{block}

\begin{block}{8 \quad Key Findings}
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep6pt
  \item \textbf{Cold-start GRPO (V3) fails.}
        Expert steering (Jazbec et al.'s stabiliser) still yields
        only 10--14\% GSM8K accuracy after 150 RL steps.
        Training correctness never rises above 0.17.

  \item \textbf{Warmstart alone recovers baseline accuracy.}
        500 BCE steps reach 84\% accuracy, matching the training-free
        DefaultPolicy. The policy has learned meaningful per-position
        decision boundaries.

  \item \textbf{Warmstart + GRPO Pareto-improves over all baselines.}
        50-sample: \textbf{86\%\,/\,57.3 TPS} vs.\
        84\%\,/\,53.1 (DefaultPolicy).
        Full-scale: \textbf{79.6\%\,/\,72.6 TPS} ---
        quality-mode accuracy at speed-mode throughput.

  \item \textbf{GRPO teaches compute-awareness.}
        Accuracy at $\tau{=}0.5$ jumps 70\%$\to$86\%
        from step 100 to 200 while TPS holds at 57--61.
        The multiplicative reward teaches \emph{selective}
        unmasking rather than uniformly fast unmasking.

  \item \textbf{Expert steering is unnecessary post-warmstart.}
        Warmstart provides the structural prior that expert steering
        was designed to inject; adding both is redundant and risks
        instability. V4/V5 use zero expert rollouts.
\end{enumerate}
\end{block}
