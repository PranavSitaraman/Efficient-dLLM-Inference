\begin{abstract}
% ======================================================================
Masked diffusion language models (MDLMs) generate text by iteratively unmasking tokens in parallel, but most inference methods still use fixed confidence thresholds or block schedules.
We propose \textbf{Any-Order Adaptive Editing (AOAE)}, a lossy self-speculative framework for MoE MDLMs with three contributions.
First, a fast hard-routed drafter accumulates cheap draft microsteps into a transient frontier $\cK_{\text{spec}}$, while a normal-quality verifier validates the frontier: accepting matching tokens, remasking rejections, and resetting the drafter cache when rejection grows high.
The composition knob $\gamma$ controls the speed--quality tradeoff; at $\gamma{=}0$ the verifier distribution is preserved and speedup comes only from accepted draft work.
Second, the learned stability cache $\cK_{\text{stable}}$ persists verified KV states across steps.
Third, GRPO trains the cache ($\kappa_t$) and access ($q_t$) heads to predict \emph{which} positions to persist and \emph{when} to refresh them.
\end{abstract}

% ======================================================================
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
% ======================================================================

Masked diffusion language models (MDLMs) generate text by reversing a token-masking process, producing all positions in parallel rather than left-to-right~\cite{austin2021structured,sahoo2024simple,shi2024simplified,lou2024discrete}.
Recent scaling has produced frontier-class MDLMs---LLaDA~2.0 at 100B parameters~\cite{nie2025llada2} and LLaDA~2.1~\cite{llada21} with Token-to-Token (T2T) editing---but inference remains governed by static heuristics: fixed confidence thresholds and rigid semi-autoregressive blocks that require per-task tuning, degrade on long sequences~\cite{jazbec2025learning}, and impose uniform compute regardless of position difficulty.

Three lines of work address these limitations. Adaptive token ordering~\cite{kim2025trainworst} and learned RL unmasking policies~\cite{jazbec2025learning} improve accuracy and efficiency, but cover only unmasking. PRISM~\cite{kim2025prism} adds self-correction via learned remasking but uses a fixed threshold. dKV-Cache~\cite{ma2025dkv} and Elastic-Cache~\cite{elastic_cache} accelerate inference by caching stable KV states but constrain the decoding trajectory to block or prefix patterns, sacrificing diffusion's any-order flexibility.

AOAE is built on a simple observation: MoE MDLMs expose a natural drafter--verifier split. With standard hard top-$k$ routing, LLaDA~2.1-mini activates ${\sim}$1.4B of 16B parameters per token; the same frozen model with normal routing acts as a high-quality verifier. The hard-routed drafter runs an aggressive inference shortcut (LLaDA S-mode, dKV/Elastic-style caching, or a learned cache policy), while the verifier periodically validates the accumulated draft frontier. High rejection triggers remasking and drafter-cache reset. This makes the verifier a natural cache-realignment mechanism: the drafter's cache is speculative, and the verifier authoritatively decides what survives.

\section{Background}
\label{sec:background}
% ======================================================================

\subsection{Masked Diffusion Models}
\label{sec:bg_mdm}

Let $\bx_0 = (x_0^1, \dots, x_0^L) \in \cV^L$ be a token sequence with vocabulary $\cV$ and mask token $\mask$.
The forward process independently corrupts each token:
\begin{equation}
    q(x_t^k \mid x_0^k) = \begin{cases}
        1 - t, & \text{if } x_t^k = x_0^k, \\
        t, & \text{if } x_t^k = \mask, \\
        0, & \text{otherwise,}
    \end{cases}
    \label{eq:forward}
\end{equation}
for $t \in [0, 1]$.
A denoising network $p_{\btheta}$ is trained via the ELBO~\cite{ou2024your,sahoo2024simple}:
\begin{equation}
    \cL(\btheta) = \E_{t,\, \bx_0,\, \bx_t} \left[ \frac{1}{t} \sum_{k=1}^L \ind[x_t^k = \mask] \log p_{\btheta}(x_0^k \mid \bx_t) \right].
    \label{eq:elbo}
\end{equation}
At inference, the model starts from $\by_T = (\mask, \dots, \mask)$; let $p_t^k := p_{\btheta}(y_0^k{=}\cdot \mid \by_t)$.
At each step $t$, a sampling strategy selects a subset $\cU_t \subseteq \cM_t$ of masked positions $\cM_t = \{k : y_t^k = \mask\}$ to unmask:
\begin{equation}
    y_{t-1}^k = \begin{cases}
        y \sim p_t^k, & \text{if } k \in \cU_t, \\
        y_t^k, & \text{otherwise.}
    \end{cases}
    \label{eq:sampling}
\end{equation}
The choice of $\cU_t$ critically affects both speed and quality~\cite{jazbec2025learning,wu2025fastdllm}.

\subsection{Related Caching, Correction, and Policy Methods}
\label{sec:bg_related}

\paragraph{KV caching.}
In MDLMs, bidirectional attention makes KV states change across denoising steps, so naive reuse can become stale.
dKV-Cache~\cite{ma2025dkv} and Elastic-Cache~\cite{elastic_cache} reuse KVs at stable positions but require blockwise or windowed access patterns that constrain the decoding trajectory.
AOAE keeps these policies plug-and-play for the drafter while giving the verifier authority to reject stale drafts and reset the fast cache.

\paragraph{Self-correction and learned policies.}
PRISM~\cite{kim2025prism} learns per-token keep/remask scores for self-correction; AOAE uses primary logits directly for validation and treats PRISM as an optional advisory feature or gated-verifier ablation.
Jazbec et al.~\cite{jazbec2025learning} train a lightweight unmasking policy with GRPO~\cite{shao2024deepseekmath}; AOAE extends this to a full speculative framework, by default training only the cache/access heads while freezing unmask/remask behavior.

\section{Method: Any-Order Adaptive Editing}
\label{sec:method}
% ======================================================================

AOAE is formalized as a finite-horizon MDP $(\cS, \cA, P, R, T)$ over a dual-model system.

\subsection{Dual-Model MoE Architecture}
\label{sec:soft_moe}

LLaDA~2.1-mini is a 16B-parameter MoE model with $E{=}128$ routed experts per layer; $k{=}8$ are selected per token via hard top-$k$ routing (${\sim}$1.4B active parameters). AOAE instantiates two operational modes of the \emph{same} frozen model:

\paragraph{Drafter (auxiliary).}
Hard-routed (${\sim}$1.4B active); may run any cheap approximate mode: LLaDA S-mode, prefix reuse, dKV/Elastic-style freezing, or a learned cache policy.
Each unmasked token is appended to a transient frontier $\cK_{\text{spec}}$ for later verification.

\paragraph{Verifier (primary).}
Runs the normal high-quality mode. The soft-routed variant replaces hard routing with temperature-controlled weights:
\begin{equation}
    w_i^{\text{soft}} = \frac{\exp(z_i / \tau_r)}{\sum_{j=1}^E \exp(z_j / \tau_r)}, \quad \tau_r \to 0 \text{ recovers hard routing.}
    \label{eq:soft_route}
\end{equation}
Both modes share frozen weights; routing is toggled in-place, avoiding two full model copies.
At each verifier event, the primary validates every stored proposal in $\cK_{\text{spec}}$: a frontier token is accepted iff it matches the primary argmax (configurable to a probability threshold or PRISM gate). Rejected tokens are remasked and evicted from $\cK_{\text{stable}}$; if the frontier rejection rate exceeds threshold $\eta$, the drafter cache $\cC_{\text{aux}}$ is reset and the verifier re-runs on the corrected state.

The canonical scheduler verifies after 12 accumulated candidate tokens or 4 draft microsteps (whichever comes first), with forced first/last events. This candidate-budget rule adapts naturally to LLaDA S-mode and Q-mode, which draft different token counts per step.

\subsection{State: Soft-Masked Representations}
\label{sec:state}

The policy's state must convey predictive uncertainty, not just binary mask indicators.
Following Hersche et al.~\cite{hersche2025softmasked}, we use a soft-masked embedding per position:
\begin{equation}
    \bh_t^k = \lambda(p_t^k)\, \mathbf{E}_{\mask} + \bigl(1 - \lambda(p_t^k)\bigr) \sum_{j \in \text{top-}K(p_t^k)} \pi_j^k\, \mathbf{E}_{v_j},
    \label{eq:softmask}
\end{equation}
where $\pi_j^k = p_t^k(v_j) / \sum_{j'} p_t^k(v_{j'})$ are renormalized top-$K$ probabilities and the gating function is:
\begin{equation}
    \lambda(p_t^k) = \omega_s \cdot \sigma\!\bigl(\omega_a(-H(p_t^k) - \omega_b)\bigr),
    \label{eq:gating}
\end{equation}
with learnable $\omega = (\omega_s, \omega_a, \omega_b)$. At low confidence (high entropy) $\lambda \to \omega_s$ and the mask embedding dominates; at high confidence $\lambda \to 0$ and token embeddings dominate.

The full state is $s_t = (\bH_t, \bfm_t, \bm{\alpha}_t, \bm{a}_t, \bm{\ell}_t, t/T)$, where $\alpha_t^k$ is the authoritative verifier acceptance at frontier positions, $a_t^k$ is a normalized cache/access age feature, and $\ell_t^k$ encodes the last positional action.
The parameters $\omega$ are trained jointly with the policy via GRPO; to propagate gradients without re-running the base model, \texttt{forward()} stores the weighted token sum $\mathbf{w}_t^k$, which \texttt{recompute\_h\_t()} uses during GRPO backward at ${\sim}$200 MB overhead per rollout group.

\subsection{Action: Four-Head Policy}
\label{sec:action}

The policy exposes four Bernoulli heads; canonical GRPO freezes unmask/remask and trains only cache/access:
\begin{align}
    u_t^k &\sim \text{Ber}\bigl(\sigma(f_{\bphi^{\text{unmask}}}(s_t)^k)\bigr), \quad \text{(Unmask)}, \label{eq:action_u} \\
    r_t^k &\sim \text{Ber}\bigl(\sigma(f_{\bphi^{\text{remask}}}(s_t)^k)\bigr), \quad \text{(Remask / evict)}, \label{eq:action_r} \\
    \kappa_t^k &\sim \text{Ber}\bigl(\sigma(f_{\bphi^{\text{cache}}}(s_t)^k)\bigr), \quad \text{(Stable Cache)}, \label{eq:action_c} \\
    q_t^k &\sim \text{Ber}\bigl(\sigma(f_{\bphi^{\text{access}}}(s_t)^k)\bigr), \quad \text{(Access / Refresh)}, \label{eq:action_z} \\
    \alpha_t^k &= \ind[\arg\max_v p_t^k(v)=\hat{y}_t^k], \quad \text{(Default verifier accept)}. \label{eq:action_alpha}
\end{align}
Validity constraints: $u_t^k = 0$ if unmasked; $r_t^k = 0$ if masked; $\kappa_t^k \cdot r_t^k = 0$ (mutually exclusive); stable commits additionally require $q_t^k = 1$ and $y^k \neq \mask$.
The joint action log-likelihood factorizes over positions and heads.
We prefer remask-then-denoise over T2T editing because T2T requires learning $p(x_0^k \mid \bx_t, y_t^k \neq \mask)$ on artificially corrupted inputs---harder than the standard masked objective and incompatible with any-order generation.

\subsection{Inference: Lossy Draft--Validate Loop}
\label{sec:transition}

\begin{algorithm}[!ht]
\caption{AOAE Draft-Frontier Inference}
\label{alg:aoae}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\State \textbf{Init:} response $\by\leftarrow(\mask,\ldots,\mask)$, frontier $\cK_{\text{spec}}\leftarrow\emptyset$, stable cache $\cK_{\text{stable}}\leftarrow\emptyset$, auxiliary cache $\cC_{\text{aux}}\leftarrow\emptyset$
\For{$t = T, T{-}1, \dots, 1$}
    \If{verifier schedule does not fire}
        \State Run drafter $d_t \leftarrow p_{\btheta}^{\text{hard}}(\cdot\mid\bx,\by;\cC_{\text{aux}})$
        \State Execute frozen/learned unmask action $u_t$; store $(k,\hat{y}^k,d_t^k)$ in $\cK_{\text{spec}}$ for each new token
        \State Continue to next microstep
    \EndIf
    \State Run verifier $p_t \leftarrow p_{\btheta}^{\text{soft}}(\cdot\mid\bx,\by)$ on the current drafted state
    \For{$(k,\hat{y}^k)\in\cK_{\text{spec}}$}
        \State $\alpha_t^k \leftarrow \ind[\arg\max_v p_t^k(v)=\hat{y}^k]$ \Comment{or configured probability/PRISM gate}
        \If{$\alpha_t^k=0$}
            \State $y^k\leftarrow\mask$; \; $\cK_{\text{stable}}\leftarrow\cK_{\text{stable}}\setminus\{k\}$
        \EndIf
    \EndFor
    \If{frontier rejection rate $>\eta$}
        \State $\cC_{\text{aux}}\leftarrow\emptyset$; rerun verifier on corrected state
    \EndIf
    \State Clear $\cK_{\text{spec}}$; build $s_t$ from verifier logits, acceptance, age, and access features
    \State Sample/execute $(u_t,r_t,\kappa_t,q_t)$; canonical GRPO freezes $u_t,r_t$ and trains $\kappa_t,q_t$
    \State Remask positions with $r_t^k=1$; evict from $\cK_{\text{stable}}$
    \State Unmask selected masked positions from $\tilde{p}_t=\textsc{Compose}(p_t,d_t,\alpha_t,\gamma)$
    \State Commit $k$ to $\cK_{\text{stable}}$ only if $\kappa_t^k=1$, $q_t^k=1$, $r_t^k=0$, and $y^k\neq\mask$
\EndFor
\State \textbf{Return} $\by$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\paragraph{Two-pool cache semantics.}
$\cK_{\text{spec}}$ accumulates freshly drafted candidates between verifier events and is cleared at each event; it represents \emph{verification debt}, not persistent reuse.
$\cK_{\text{stable}}$ is populated only post-verification when $\kappa_t^k{=}1$, $q_t^k{=}1$, $r_t^k{=}0$, and evicted by remask or verifier rejection; it is the \emph{learned persistent cache}.
Conflating the two would corrupt the $\kappa_t$ training signal, so evaluation reports these pools separately: frontier occupancy, stable-cache occupancy, acceptance/rejection rates, drafter cache resets, and effective FLOPs.

The verifier's prompt KV cache is prefilled once and reused throughout. The drafter carries its own fast-path cache $\cC_{\text{aux}}$, which the verifier can clear when rejection spikes, making speculative validation a natural cache-realignment mechanism. Following~\cite{jazbec2025learning}, if $\bu_t = \mathbf{0}$ and $\cM_t \neq \emptyset$, the highest-confidence masked position is unmasked as a fallback (disabled during training to prevent reward hacking).

\subsection{Composed Prediction}
\label{sec:composed}

We compose the drafter's distribution $d_t^k$ with the verifier's $p_t^k$:
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{p}_t^k(v) \propto p_t^k(v) \cdot d_t^k(v)^{\gamma \cdot \alpha_t^k},
    \label{eq:composed}
\end{equation}
where $\gamma \geq 0$ controls composition strength and $\alpha_t^k$ is the verifier acceptance mask.
At $\gamma=0$ the distribution is exactly the verifier distribution, recovering conservative speculation: quality is governed by the primary and speedup comes only from accepted draft work.
At $\gamma>0$ the composed distribution concentrates on tokens both models favor where the verifier agrees, while the any-order property is preserved by falling back to the verifier on rejection.

\subsection{Policy Architecture}
\label{sec:architecture}

The steering policy $\pi_{\bphi}$ is a lightweight transformer:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
    \item \textbf{Input:} Per-position features $(\bh_t^k, m_t^k, \alpha_t^k, a_t^k, \ell_t^k, t/T)$ plus an optional PRISM score, projected to dimension $d_\pi$.
    \item \textbf{Backbone:} $N_\pi$-layer transformer with $d_\pi$-dimensional hidden states and full bidirectional attention.
    \item \textbf{Heads:} Four linear projections for unmask, remask, stable cache ($\kappa_t$), and access/refresh ($q_t$) logits per position.
\end{enumerate}
With $N_\pi = 1$ and $d_\pi = 128$, this is ${\sim}$500K parameters ($<$0.01\% of the base model).
Age features and last-action encodings allow the access head to model temporal revisit patterns from remasking and cache resets.

\subsection{Positional Speculative Caching}
\label{sec:positional_spec_cache}

The $\kappa_t$ head predicts which positions are stable enough to persist in KV cache; the $q_t$ head predicts which positions will need compute soon and should be refreshed.
The executed access set $\cQ_t$ includes all positions modified at step $t$ (mandatory) plus a top-$B$ speculative set predicted from state:
\begin{equation}
    \cQ_t = \text{Top-}B\bigl(\{f_{\bphi^{\text{access}}}(s_t)^k\}_{k=1}^L\bigr) \cup \{k : u_t^k = 1 \lor r_t^k = 1\}.
    \label{eq:Qt_candidates}
\end{equation}
Unlike fixed windows or block schedules, $\cQ_t$ is predicted from the current state rather than prescribed by position, treating caching as an \emph{access-pattern prediction problem}: allocate refresh budget to positions likely to change soon without forcing the unmask/remask trajectory to become left-to-right or blockwise.
This mechanism is model-agnostic and compatible with any MDLM that exposes token-wise uncertainty.

\subsection{Training via GRPO}
\label{sec:optimization}

The base model remains frozen. Canonical GRPO trains only the cache ($\kappa_t$) and access ($q_t$) heads; full four-head training and trainable soft-mask gates are retained as ablations.

\paragraph{Reward.}
Let $c_{\text{aux}}$ be the relative cost of one drafter microstep and $m_t$ the verifier miss fraction after stable-cache reuse:
\begin{equation}
    \text{eff\_flops}
    =
    \frac{c_{\text{aux}} N_{\text{aux}} + \sum_{t\in\cV_{\text{verify}}} m_t}{T},
    \label{eq:mean_cached}
\end{equation}
where $T$ is the cost of running the verifier at every microstep.
The reward is:
\begin{equation}
    R = r(\by^*, \hat{\by}) \cdot (1 - \text{eff\_flops})^\alpha
      - \beta\, \overline{\text{Thrash}}
      - w_u\, f_{\text{unresolved}}
      + w_{F1}\, \overline{F1}_{\text{cache}}
      + w_q\, F1_{\text{access}},
    \label{eq:reward}
\end{equation}
where $r(\cdot)$ is task correctness; $\overline{\text{Thrash}}$ penalizes remasking stable-cached positions only; $f_{\text{unresolved}}$ penalizes leftover $\mask$ tokens; $\overline{F1}_{\text{cache}}$ is a soft precision-recall signal rewarding $\kappa_t$ for committing low-drift positions; and $F1_{\text{access}}$ measures whether $\cQ_t$ anticipates actual modifications over the next $H$ steps.
The multiplicative speed term ensures incorrect generations receive zero reward regardless of speed, preventing the reward hacking observed with additive formulations~\cite{jazbec2025learning}.

\paragraph{GRPO objective.}
For each prompt, $G$ trajectories are sampled under $\pi_{\bphi_{\text{old}}}$; with advantage $A^g = R^g - \bar{R}$ and importance ratio $\rho_t^g = \pi_{\bphi}(\mathbf{a}_t^g \mid s_t^g) / \pi_{\bphi_{\text{old}}}(\mathbf{a}_t^g \mid s_t^g)$:
\begin{equation}
    \cJ(\bphi) = \E_{\bx, \by^*} \left[ \frac{1}{G} \sum_{g=1}^G \frac{1}{T - \hat{T}_g} \sum_{t=\hat{T}_g}^{T} \min\!\Big\{ \rho_t^g A^g,\; \text{clip}(\rho_t^g, 1{-}\epsilon, 1{+}\epsilon)\, A^g \Big\} \right].
    \label{eq:grpo}
\end{equation}
KL regularization is omitted since the policy is trained from scratch. In practice, log-ratios are clamped before exponentiation, advantages are normalized by within-group standard deviation, and frozen deterministic heads are excluded from the likelihood ratio.

\section{Experimental Design}
\label{sec:experiments}
% ======================================================================

\paragraph{Setup.}
LLaDA~2.1-mini (16B MoE, frozen) in dual-model configuration.
Policy: 1-layer transformer with $d_\pi{=}128$ (${\sim}$500K parameters).
Canonical configuration: candidate budget 12, max 4 draft microsteps, forced first/last verifier events, argmax-match acceptance.
Hyperparameters: $K{=}5$, $G{=}8$, $\alpha{=}1.0$, $\beta{=}0.1$, $\epsilon{=}0.2$, $\gamma{=}0.5$, $c_{\text{aux}}{=}0.35$, $w_{F1}{=}0.02$, $w_u{=}0.25$, $w_q{=}0$ initially.
GRPO trains cache/access heads by default; full four-head training is an ablation.

\paragraph{Datasets.}
GSM8K~\cite{cobbe2021gsm8k} and MATH~\cite{hendrycks2021math} (mathematical reasoning); HumanEval~\cite{chen2021humaneval} (code generation).
Policy training uses 50K prompts from OpenMathInstruct-2.

\paragraph{Metrics.}
Accuracy (exact match / pass@1), throughput (tokens/sec), NFEs, verifier-call rate, draft microsteps per step, frontier size, frontier acceptance/rejection rates, drafter cache resets, stable-cache occupancy, access F1, effective FLOPs, and refresh ratio $\frac{1}{T}\sum_t |\cQ_t|/L$.

\paragraph{Ablations.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=1pt]
    \item \textbf{Speculation:} verifier-only; drafter-only; frontier validation; fixed-step vs.\ candidate-budget scheduling; $\gamma=0$ conservative composition.
    \item \textbf{Caching:} $\cK_{\text{spec}}$ only; $\cK_{\text{stable}}$ only; combined; learned vs.\ heuristic drafter cache; cache-reset thresholds.
    \item \textbf{Verifier:} primary argmax; probability threshold; PRISM advisory; PRISM-gated acceptance.
    \item \textbf{Policy:} cache/access-only training; full four-head training; no agreement feature; no access head; $N_\pi \in \{1,2,4\}$.
\end{itemize}
