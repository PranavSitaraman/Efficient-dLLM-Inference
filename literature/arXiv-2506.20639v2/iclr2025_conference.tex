% Diffusion large language models (dLLMs) are compelling alternatives to autoregressive (AR) models, especially for code generation due to their capacity for global planning. 
% % However, the training recipes tailored towards dLLMs remain under-explored compared to recipes for AR models. 
% To unlock the potential of open dLLMs for coding and demystify their decoding behavior, we systematically investigate both effective training pipelines and interpretable denoising processes. 
% In this work, we introduce \textbf{DiffuCoder}, a 7B dLLM specialized for code generation that achieves SOTA performance among open-sourced dLLMs. 
% We provide a full training pipeline spanning pre-, mid-, and post-training.
% We also analyze the decoding behavior of dLLMs, revealing how they behave differently from AR models: increasing sampling temperature diversifies not only token choices but also their generation order. This diversity creates a rich search space, laying the groundwork for effective RL exploration.
% During RL training, to reduce the variance of token log-likelihood estimates and maintain RL training efficiency, we propose \textbf{coupled-GRPO}, a novel sampling scheme that couples the masks of examples used in training. 
% In our experiments, coupled-GRPO further improves DiffuCoder's performance on code generation benchmarks (+4.4\% on EvalPlus) without reliance on semi-AR decoding.
% Our work provides a strong open-source model, a deeper insight into the machinery of dLLM generation, and an effective, diffusion-native training framework. \url{https://github.com/apple/ml-diffucoder}
